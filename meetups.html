Denver Script, 27 February, 2018. 6pm to 9pm.

People I Met:

Stephen - Full Stack (but mostly Front-End) Developer at machineshop.io

Shannon B - Full Stack Developer @ Cake (Work w/ Pinnacle Insurance)

What I Learned:

Very long and involved talk about how useful Docker has become over Vagrant for virtual machines, specifically the strain on memory usage. I found it quite interesting, though several bits of terminology, and the technology itself, went a little over my head. There also was a talk from the perspective of someone who has worked in tech for quite some time, and the experiences they have gone through in regards to various jobs. Their presentation was a kind of "pick your own adventure" game type deal, which definitely helped with keeping attention for the audience as well as getting them more involved. Overall, a very interesting talk from both presenters. Also, interesting side note, when on Github, if you press 'T' on the keyboard you can search all of your Github files.

Questions I Have:

1. Im a little lost when it comes to the actual use of Docker as a whole, though I guess we will be going over that at some point in the curriculum according to other students.
2. What is the reason that Vagrant is so intensive on memory and how does Docker get around that?
3. How realistic is it that the second speaker went through so much crap in her career? Will I go through anything like that?
